INSTRUCTIONS

1. These interrogatories are continuing in nature under Minn. R. Civ. P. 33.01.
2. If you object to any interrogatory, state the basis for the objection with
   particularity and answer to the extent the interrogatory is not objectionable.

DEFINITIONS

1. "Document" means any writing as defined in Minn. R. Civ. P. 34.01.
2. "You" and "Your" mean Defendant Granite Supply Co. and its agents.


INTERROGATORY NO. 1: State the full legal name, business address, and principal place
of business of Defendant.

INTERROGATORY NO. 2: Describe in detail the manufacturing process used to produce the
500 granite widget assemblies at issue in this action.

INTERROGATORY NO. 3: State the date on which each of the assemblies was tendered for
delivery to Plaintiff.

INTERROGATORY NO. 4: Identify every quality-control inspection performed on the
assemblies before shipment, including the date and the person who performed it.

INTERROGATORY NO. 5: For each assembly that You contend conformed to Specification
GS-11 at the time of tender, identify:
(a) the serial or lot number of the assembly;
(b) the inspector who certified its conformity; and
(c) the document that records the inspection result.

INTERROGATORY NO. 6: State whether You contend that any nonconformity arose after
tender, and if so, describe the factual basis for that contention.

INTERROGATORY NO. 7: Identify all communications between You and Plaintiff concerning
the condition of the assemblies.

INTERROGATORY NO. 8: State the amount of any refund, credit, or price adjustment You
have offered to Plaintiff, and the date of each such offer.
